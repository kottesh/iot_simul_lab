## AIM

To simulate the odometer(distance calculator) 

## APPARATUS REQUIRED

- Arduino Uno
- Breadboard
- Stepper Motor (NEMA 17)
- A4988 Stepper Motor Driver
- I2C LCD Display (16x2)
- Pushbuttons (x4) (Start/Stop, Increase Speed, Decrease Speed, Reset)
- Jumper wires

## PRE-CONDITIONS

- The stepper motor, A4988 driver module, pushbuttons, and I2C LCD are wired securely to the Arduino.
- The A4988 driver is connected with proper microstepping pins configured (MS1, MS2, MS3) and appropriate power.
- The `Wire.h` and `LiquidCrystal_I2C.h` libraries are installed in the Arduino IDE.
- The code is successfully compiled and uploaded to the Arduino Uno board.

## POST-CONDITIONS

- The stepper motor acts as a wheel simulator, turning at the specified target speed.
- The LCD accurately displays the simulated speed (m/s) and total distance traveled (m).
- The pushbuttons effectively start/stop the motor, adjust the simulated target speed, and reset the distance counter.
- System handles speed adjustments gracefully without sudden jumps.
