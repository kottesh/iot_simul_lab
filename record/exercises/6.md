## AIM

To simulate the gas leakage detection 

## APPARATUS REQUIRED

- Arduino Uno
- Gas Sensor (MQ Series)
- RGB LED
- Buzzer
- Relay Module
- I2C OLED Display (128x64)
- 200-ohm Resistors (x3)
- Jumper wires

## PRE-CONDITIONS

- The Gas Sensor, RGB LED, Buzzer, Relay module, and I2C OLED display are correctly wired to the Arduino.
- Libraries `Wire.h`, `Adafruit_GFX.h`, and `Adafruit_SSD1306.h` are installed in the Arduino IDE.
- The code is successfully compiled and uploaded to the Arduino board.
- The sensor has had appropriate time to preheat if dealing with a real hardware MQ sensor.

## POST-CONDITIONS

- The OLED screen displays the real-time gas concentration values and system status (SAFE, WARNING, CRITICAL).
- In a SAFE state, the RGB LED glows green, the buzzer is off, and the relay (exhaust) is deactivated.
- In a WARNING state, the RGB LED glows yellow and the buzzer beeps intermittently.
- In a CRITICAL state, the RGB LED glows red, the buzzer sounds a continuous alarm, and the relay activates the exhaust fan.
