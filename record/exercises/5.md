## AIM

To simulate the motion detection and usage of photo resistor 

## APPARATUS REQUIRED

- Arduino Uno
- Breadboard
- PIR Motion Sensor
- Photoresistor (LDR) Sensor Module
- LEDs (x3) (White, Cyan, Orange)
- 220-ohm Resistors (x3)
- Jumper wires

## PRE-CONDITIONS

- The PIR sensor, LDR module, and LEDs (with resistors) are correctly wired to the designated Arduino pins.
- The LDR module's analog (AO) and digital (DO) outputs are properly connected.
- The code is successfully compiled and uploaded to the Arduino Uno board.
- Serial Monitor is opened at 9600 baud rate to view the debug messages.

## POST-CONDITIONS

- The White LED activates when the PIR sensor detects motion.
- The Cyan LED's brightness dynamically adjusts (PWM) based on the ambient light detected by the LDR's analog pin.
- The Orange LED activates exclusively when the LDR's digital threshold drops indicating a dark environment.
- The Serial Monitor displays continuous real-time status of motion detection, calculated lux levels, and system diagnostics.
